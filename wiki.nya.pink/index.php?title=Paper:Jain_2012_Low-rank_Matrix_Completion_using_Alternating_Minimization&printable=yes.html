Notes for paper "Low-rank Matrix Completion using Alternating Minimization" by Prateek Jain, Praneeth Netrapalli, and Sujay Sanghavi.

arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/1212.0467

PDF: https://arxiv.org/pdf/1212.0467.pdf

Abstract

Alternating minimization is an approach to solving for best-fit low-rank matrices.

In this approach, the target low-rank matrix is written in bi-linear form. Then the algorithm alters between finding the best U {\displaystyle U} and best V {\displaystyle V} .

In isolation, each alternating step is convex and tractable but the overall problem is not convex

Open problem: Forming a theory of when the approach yields a "good" result.

This paper presents theoretical analysis of the performance for alternating minimization for matrix completion, and for matrix sensing. It also shows that alternating minimization guarantees geometric convergence to the true matrix.

Introduction

In low-rank matrix fitting, the target rank k {\displaystyle k} matrix X ∈ R m × n {\displaystyle X\in \mathbb {R} ^{m\times n}} is parametrized in bi-linear form X = U V † {\displaystyle X=UV^{\dagger }} , where U ∈ R m × k {\displaystyle U\in \mathbb {R} ^{m\times k}} and V ∈ R n × k {\displaystyle V\in \mathbb {R} ^{n\times k}} .

This paper presents guarantees for the performance of alternating minimization for matrix completion and matrix sensing.

Matrix completion is the problem of completing a low-rank matrix from a few of its entries.

Matrix sensing is the problem of recovering a low-rank matrix M ∈ R m × n {\displaystyle M\in \mathbb {R} ^{m\times n}} from affine equations. Given linear measurements b i = t r ( A i † M ) {\displaystyle b_{i}=tr(A_{i}^{\dagger }M)} and measurement matrices A i {\displaystyle A_{i}} , recover M {\displaystyle M} .

Note: Matrix completion is a special case of matrix sensing where each obsered entry in the matrix completion problem is one single-element measurement matrix A i {\displaystyle A_{i}} .

Both problems are in general NP-hard without any other conditions. What are the similar conditions to the ones used by the existing methods? Apparently alternating minimization guarantees recovery of the true matrix but do the conditions always guarantee that only one true matrix exists?
